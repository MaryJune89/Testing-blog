In my 30s, I decided that social work was no longer for me. I had reached my peak in the field. I realized about mid way through studying for my LISW that I did not want to be a therapist or counselor. I felt stagnant and I was not happy in the role that I was in. It just wasn't challenging enough & the workload heavily outweighed the pay.

During the COVID-19 Pandemic, I had been moved to work remote. I also had my second child during that time and that marked a huge change in my life. I knew that my life was in for a shift and things would not be the same. I also decided that if I can, I want to continue to work from home or hybrid. I enjoyed the flexibility and time I was able to spend with my family. So, I decided to embark on the path to become a Software Engineer.

As I began to research ways to become a Software Engineer, I decided to dive into coding. I enrolled in a free boot camp and decided discovered that my interests were in Frontend Web Development. Throughout my path, I started to work on small projects as I was learning, including this very blog that you're reading. I loved the process of solving complex problems in addition to creating content that was easy for users to access. I discovered a community that was more than willing to help me throughout my learning process. I would have never discovered all this, had I not taken that leap of faith. If you have been thinking on beginning your path, do not wait! Start now!

I knew that I had to make a plan. Below is a list of some things I put in order before starting my tech journey.
  • Research your tech interests.
  • Review multiple sources of information.
  • Take your time and develop a learning path strategy that fits your lifestyle.
  • Take things little by little, try not to overwhelm yourself.
  • Find a community to join for support.
  • Start, always use that motivated energy and just start.
